Week 16 - Knick Knacks Kick Dirt

Overview Gallery Item Data Fun Facts Item of the Week Wrapping Up

Welcome back!

Embarking on a project to record and share each week of my trash was driven by a blend of curiosity and a desire to understand my own lifestyle habits. This eventually evolved into a personal challenge to see what my waste could reveal about the things I frequently use and discard.

The focus was on non-food items, steering clear of the usual narrative around food and drink containers. I was intrigued by the other stuff – junk, old tech gadgets, and various household items.

Each week, as I documented these items, patterns began to emerge. It was fascinating to see how the things I threw away painted a picture of my consumption habits. Sharing this journey online added another dimension to the project. The process has become a creative outlet, allowing me to connect with others over the shared experiences of everyday life and the items we use and discard.

This project turned into an exercise in looking at the small, often overlooked aspects of daily life and understanding their larger implications. It’s also highlighted the importance of being aware of our consumption patterns and their impact on our lives and the environment.

Overview

A Diverse Mix of Memories and Technology

This week's disposal activity was a tapestry of diverse items, each narrating a unique story. Unlike previous weeks, I found myself surprised by the emotional bonds I had formed with these objects. Their disposal became more than a routine task; it offered unique insights into the lifecycle of my possessions and the reasons behind their redundancy.

Tech and Gadgets: Evolving with Time

The 'Tech and Gadgets' category was particularly telling. It included my original Nintendo Switch console, a beloved companion in gaming that I recently upgraded. Selling the original to a family friend, I kept all accessories for the new OLED model. Then, there was the USB-to-USB extender cable - a relic from my electronics collection, now grimy and obsolete. And the microphone arm, a faithful partner in my music-making journey, finally replaced after years of service. These items highlighted my evolving relationship with technology and the inevitable obsolescence of gadgets.

Personal Accessories: Phases of Utility

In the 'Personal Accessories' category, each item marked different phases of my hectic student life. The Tacoria aluminum reusable water bottle was once a daily essential and recycled after sitting unused. The Rutgers Mason Gross branded sunglasses were a memento from a past event and were given away to declutter. Lastly, the black fidget cube, damaged and no longer functional, was discarded. These objects reflected the changing nature of what I find useful or meaningful.

Decorative and Miscellaneous: The Art of Letting Go

Finally, the 'Decorative and Miscellaneous' items included desk ornaments and other junk that I cleared after varying. The smiley face sand bottle, once a cherished desk ornament, was donated as it no longer matched my decor. An old Post-It note, its purpose long forgotten, was thrown away. These items represented my shifting decorative preferences and the simplicity of everyday objects.

Conclusion: More Than Just Disposal

This week's disposal activity was a microcosm of life's transitions. Each item, from tech gadgets to personal accessories and decorative pieces, narrated a chapter of my life. This project took form as a reflection on the fluid nature of our possessions and the enduring memories they encapsulate.

Profile: Nintendo Switch Console

Why the Nintendo Switch?

In the realm of gaming and social connectivity, few gaming devices have made an impact quite like the Nintendo Switch. It revived the aging handheld form factor, breathing life into portable gaming after years of over-monetized mobile game domination. This week, it takes the spotlight as my 'Item of the Week' - a tribute to its role in my recent years and my current transition to its successor, the Switch OLED model.

A Personal Journey with the Switch

I welcomed the Nintendo Switch into my life shortly after its 2017 release. It was a gateway to immersive worlds, and quickly filled a huge role in my life. Animal Crossing and Let's Build a Zoo became my go-to titles, offering countless hours of serene gameplay.

But the real magic happened with multiplayer games like "Super Smash Bros." and "Mario Kart", which turned the Switch into a social hub, creating unforgettable moments with friends and family. These experiences transcended the digital realm, fostering real connections with my loved ones and new friends.

As someone who seldom upgrades tech unless absolutely needed, the decision to switch to the OLED model was pretty significant. It marked the end of an era with my original Switch, a testament to the console's lasting impact on my gaming habits.

The Switch: Beyond Gaming

The Switch's hybrid design revolutionized the gaming industry. It bridged the gap between home consoles and portable devices, redefining how we interact with games and each other. Its launch was not necessarily a technological breakthrough (despite its novel form factor), but it was certainly a cultural phenomenon.

The Nintendo Switch has been a companion in both solitary and shared gaming adventures. Its evolution with the OLED model mirrors the dynamic lifecycle of technology and its impact on our social and environmental landscape. This week, as I bid farewell to my original Switch, I celebrate its mileage and the countless memories it has created.
